How the parallax hero image works

Many parallax sites do not work on iOS devices. This is usually because they are using background-attachement: fixed to achieve a stationary background image. Unfortunately iOS does not render background-attachement: fixed for performance related reasons. Instead it treats is as a normal background image which moves with the rest of the content.

The trick I have used here is to place the hero image as a background to a div which has position: fixed which fixes the container, rather than the background image. The content (Usually the page title) would normally be contained inside the div with the background-attached image, but in this case the text is centred in a seperate div with the same size as the fixed hero image. By using viewport units (100vw 100vh) the whole hero element is made responsive.

Nor again is there anyone who loves or pursues or desires to obtain pain of itself, because it is pain, but because occasionally circumstances occur in which toil and pain can procure him some great pleasure.

To take a trivial example, which of us ever undertakes laborious physical exercise, except to obtain some advantage from it? But who has any right to find fault with a man who chooses to enjoy a pleasure that has no annoying consequences, or one who avoids a pain that produces no resultant pleasure?

On the other hand, we denounce with righteous indignation and dislike men who are so beguiled and demoralized by the charms of pleasure of the moment, so blinded by desire, that they cannot foresee the pain and trouble that are bound to ensue; and equal blame belongs to those who fail in their duty through weakness of will, which is the same as saying through shrinking from toil and pain.

These cases are perfectly simple and easy to distinguish. In a free hour, when our power of choice is untrammelled and when nothing prevents our being able to do what we like best, every pleasure is to be welcomed and every pain avoided.

But in certain circumstances and owing to the claims of duty or the obligations of business it will frequently occur that pleasures have to be repudiated and annoyances accepted.

The wise man therefore always holds in these matters to this principle of selection: he rejects pleasures to secure other greater pleasures, or else he endures pains to avoid worse pains. But I must explain to you how all this mistaken idea ofdenouncing pleasure and praising pain was born and I will give you a complete account of the system, and expound the actual teachings of the great explorer of the truth.
