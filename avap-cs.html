Case study

Building games that teach

Role

UX Director, EVERFI

Why this project is important to me

By 2020, data showed that vaping had erased decades of anti-tobacco progress with teens. Partnering with the Truth Initiative in January of 2020, I led a team of designers, researchers, writers, and developers tasked with creating a dynamic course that showed high school students the dangers of vaping. Working with teens from Students Working Against Tobacco (SWAT), we developed an experience and message that addressed the realities of nicotine addiction and unmasked the marketing practices of vaping companies.

TL;DR: working with teens to tackle vaping

Discovery

We met virtually with teens from SWAT and they shared their perspectives on teen vaping. We also surveyed high school teachers and students around the US.

Findings

Data revealed most teens, like most people at the time, didn't find vaping dangerous or addictive. Early user tests with draft content showed presenting data and facts on the dangers of vaping resonated with teens.

Next steps

Unique perspectives culled from research generated new ideas. The content team focused on exposing the similarities between tobacco and vaping companies. Prototypes of a card game tested well.

Validate

Further testing confirmed the impact of the changes. Shorter text blocks, interactivity that deepened learning, and personal stories from peers not only increased engagement, but changed minds.

Build

The findings from research and discovery reduced development time and bugs. There were no major pivots or rebuilds and testing helped gain insights that impacted our entire product suite.

Project Details

Our findings revealed that most teens knew cigarettes were dangerous, but thought vaping was safe, even healthy. Also, few considered vaping addictive. With a course to build, a tight deadline, and people counting on us to get it right, we were able to deliver because we:

  • Had a research-driven process
  • Developed with design components
  • Validated with customers and users

Discovery

We met with the research team from Truth on multiple occasions to share data, create personas, draft surveys, and develop lo-fi content-focused prototypes to test. Our intitial findings were:

  • Personal stories about addiction from peers resonated with teens
  • Screens with long text blocks promoted skimming, reducing engagement and retention
  • Interactivity promoted user engagement, but needed to be more than just delightful

This prompted the team to explore the parallels in the marketing practices of tobacco and vaping companies to help expose misinformation. We also worked with teens who were willing to share their nicotine addiction story with others. And we decided to rethink our assessment model and introduce a card game to measure retention.

Development

The team had developed two proven tools for building effective learning experiences:

  • Courses that told character-driven stories
  • A fully-developed design system that was mirrored in the code

We relied on these tools to rapidly prototype and test ideas with SWAT, inspiring creative development, shorter text, and clear UI. After multiple rounds of testing, we found a solution that was accessible, responsive, and fun.

Validate

The course launched without issue and was available in every high school classroom in the United States in November of 2020. The course received the 2021 Silver Halo Award.
